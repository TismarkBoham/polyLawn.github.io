The polyculture principle

Conventional lawns are monocultures — a single species optimised for appearance but requiring constant inputs: fertiliser, herbicide, frequent mowing, irrigation. Polyculture lawns combine nitrogen-fixing legumes, drought-resistant grasses, and low-growing wildflowers to create a self-sustaining ecosystem.

Dutch white clover fixes atmospheric nitrogen, feeding grasses without fertiliser. Fine fescues require half the mowing of Kentucky bluegrass. Yarrow and thyme fill spaces that weeds would otherwise colonise. The result: a lawn that works harder with far less from you.
